Bybit vs OKX VIP Fees

A source-bounded comparison of two complete public futures VIP schedules. The useful difference is not a universal winner: Bybit splits standard and API-heavy Pro qualification, while OKX applies the highest tier reached by assets or a single product-line volume.

Decision boundary

OKX is lower in the three zero-asset scenarios; Bybit's high-API path narrows the gap

At a 70% maker / 30% taker mix with zero qualifying assets, OKX reaches Regular, VIP 2 and VIP 3 at $1M, $10M and $100M; Bybit's standard path reaches VIP 0, VIP 1 and VIP 4. If Bybit API share exceeds 20% at $100M, Pro 1 narrows the modeled gap versus OKX VIP 3 from $2,600 to $1,200.

Important limit: This is a public-rate scenario, not an account quote. Bybit says actual rates can vary by region; OKX separates Group 1 and Group 2 and not every pair is available in every jurisdiction. Confirm both on the applicable local page or verified account.

Regular / API / VIP paths

Qualification logic differs; tier names are not interchangeable

Regular path

Bybit VIP 0 is 0.02% maker / 0.055% taker; OKX Regular is 0.02% / 0.05%. Promotions, referrals, token discounts and local-entity differences are excluded.

API

Bybit uses a 20% API trading-share boundary: $100M derivatives volume reaches VIP 4 on the standard route (about $18,000) or Pro 1 above 20% (about $16,600). OKX does not list API use alone as an automatic discount condition.

FAQ

How to use this VIP comparison

Which is cheaper at $10M monthly volume, Bybit or OKX?

With zero qualifying assets, Bybit API share at 20% or less, and a 70% maker / 30% taker mix, Bybit VIP 1 models to about $2,460 and OKX VIP 2 to about $2,130. The $330 gap applies only to the stated products and assumptions.

Why does the $100M scenario split Bybit by API share?

Bybit routes volume qualification at 20% API share or less to standard VIP and above 20% to Pro. At $100M, those paths are VIP 4 and Pro 1, modeling to about $18,000 and $16,600 at 70/30; OKX VIP 3 models to about $15,400.

Can an OKX VIP level and a Bybit VIP level be matched by name?

No. Thresholds, asset routes, product-volume definitions and API conditions differ. The page places the official ladders side by side but does not treat same-numbered tiers as equivalent accounts.

Do Bybit Pro 3 and higher rates apply to every USDT perpetual?

No. The Pro 3 through Pro 6 figures use Bybit's published top 72 USDT Perpetual group. Other USDT perpetuals can have a higher taker rate and should be confirmed on My Fee Rate.

Does this page include funding, slippage or market-maker programs?

No. Scenarios cover published maker/taker execution fees only. Funding, spread, slippage, liquidation fees, referrals, token discounts, promotions, market-maker programs and account-specific rates are excluded.
