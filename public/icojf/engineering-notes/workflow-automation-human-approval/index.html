Workflow Automation With Human Approval

Good automation removes repetitive work while keeping high-impact decisions visible, reversible, and attributable.

What can be automatic

Stable, repetitive, low-risk steps are good candidates: spreadsheet cleanup, scheduled reports, status sync, reminders, file routing, and internal notifications.

What should stay reviewed

  • Payment-related actions.
  • Deletion or bulk updates.
  • Customer-visible messages.
  • Sensitive data export.
  • Any step where the business rule still depends on judgment.

Approval design

A review screen should show the input, proposed action, related records, reviewer, timestamp, and rollback or correction path. Approval is part of the system state, not a separate chat message.

Exception queue

Failures should not disappear into email or logs only. An exception queue lets the owner review, retry, skip, correct, or annotate the failed item.
